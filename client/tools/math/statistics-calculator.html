About Statistics

Mean: The average of a set of numbers.
Median: The middle value when the numbers are sorted.
Mode: The number(s) that appear most frequently.
Range: The difference between the largest and smallest values.
Variance: The average of the squared differences from the mean.
Standard Deviation: The square root of the variance.

Formulas:
  • Mean: μ = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) / n
  • Median: Middle value (or average of two middle values if n is even)
  • Mode: Most frequent value(s)
  • Range: max(x) - min(x)
  • Variance: σ² = Σ(xᵢ - μ)² / n
  • Standard Deviation: σ = √σ²
Use Cases:
  • Summarizing and analyzing data sets
  • Comparing distributions
  • Descriptive statistics in research and business

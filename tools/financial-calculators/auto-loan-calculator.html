📚 What Is an Auto Loan?

A vehicle loan is a secured installment loan used to purchase a car, bike, or other transport. The vehicle serves as collateral, typically resulting in lower interest rates than unsecured loans. You repay in fixed monthly installments over a set term — usually 24 to 84 months.

Key components affecting your vehicle loan cost:

  • Vehicle Price: The negotiated or listed price of the vehicle.
  • Down Payment & Exchange-In: These reduce the financed amount, lowering monthly payment and total interest.
  • Registration & Insurance: One-time costs often included in the financed amount vary by state and insurer.
  • Interest Rate (APR): Determines total borrowing cost. Rates vary based on lender, vehicle age, loan term, and your repayment history.
  • Loan Term: Longer terms reduce monthly payment but increase total interest paid significantly.

📋 How to Use This Calculator

  • Vehicle Price: Enter the negotiated ex-showroom price. Negotiating even ₹50,000 off can save significant interest.
  • Down Payment: Upfront cash. A higher down payment (typically 20%+) reduces EMI and total interest.
  • Exchange-In Value: If trading in an old vehicle, enter its estimated value to reduce the financed amount.
  • Registration/Insurance: Estimate one-time costs as a percentage to include in total financing.
  • Interest Rate (APR): Check rates from banks or NBFCs. Typical vehicle loan rates range from 6-9% depending on credit profile and vehicle type.
  • Loan Term: Standard terms are 36, 48, 60, or 72 months. Shorter terms have higher EMI but lower total interest.

🧪 Auto Loan Formula

Monthly car payments follow the standard amortization formula used for fixed-rate installment loans:

Monthly Payment
M = P × [ r × (1 + r)n (1 + r)n − 1 ]
M = Monthly Payment
P = Loan Principal
r = Monthly Rate (APR ÷ 12)
n = Total Months

The principal (P) is calculated as: Vehicle Price + Sales Tax − Down Payment − Trade-in Value. Each monthly payment covers both interest and principal, with early payments being mostly interest.

💡 Practical Example

You’re buying a ₹800,000 vehicle with a ₹160,000 down payment, no exchange-in, 5% registration/insurance, at 8% APR for 60 months.

Registration/Insurance: ₹800,000 × 5% = ₹40,000. Financed amount: ₹800,000 + ₹40,000 − ₹160,000 = ₹680,000.

Your monthly EMI would be approximately ₹15,400. Over 60 months you’d pay ₹924,000 total, meaning ₹244,000 in interest.

If you opted for a 48-month term instead, your EMI rises to ~₹16,200/month, but you’d save ₹60,000+ in interest. This shows why shorter terms save on total cost when monthly budgets allow.

❓ Auto Loan FAQ

Should I choose a longer or shorter loan term?

+

Shorter terms (36-48 months) have higher monthly payments but save thousands in interest. Longer terms (60-84 months) are more affordable monthly but cost significantly more overall. Choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford.

How does my credit score affect the rate?

+

Credit scores significantly impact auto loan rates. Excellent credit (750+) can get rates of 3-5%, while fair credit (600-699) may see 7-12%. Poor credit (below 600) could face 12-20% or higher. Even a 1-2% rate difference can mean thousands in savings.

Is it better to pay more down or get a lower rate?

+

Both help, but a lower interest rate typically saves more over the life of the loan. A larger down payment also protects you from negative equity. Ideally, combine both: put 20% down and shop for the best rate from multiple lenders.

Should I finance through the dealer or my bank?

+

Get pre-approval from your bank, NBFC, or credit union first to know your maximum borrowing capacity. Compare rates; lenders often offer competitive options. Watch for hidden charges like documentation fees and insurance premiums.

Can I pay off my auto loan early?

+

Most vehicle loans allow early payoff, but check for prepayment penalties. Making extra payments toward principal — even ₹2,000-5,000 extra per month — can reduce your loan tenure and save on interest.

📐 Savings Growth Formula

FV = PV × (1 + r/100)n

Here, FV is future value, PV is present value, r is annual return rate, and n is years. Compound interest helps money grow faster over longer periods. To estimate real purchasing power, compare this nominal growth with inflation-adjusted returns.

❓ Savings Calculator FAQs

Why does compound interest matter so much?+
Compound interest earns returns on both your principal and past interest. Over long periods, this snowball effect can significantly increase your final corpus.
How does inflation impact my savings target?+
Inflation reduces purchasing power each year. A goal that seems enough today may be inadequate later, so inflation-adjusted values are essential for realistic planning.
What is a real return?+
Real return is your investment return after subtracting inflation. It reflects true growth in purchasing power, not just nominal portfolio gains.
How important is the investment time horizon?+
Time horizon is critical because compounding accelerates with time. Starting earlier generally lowers the monthly amount needed to reach the same future target.

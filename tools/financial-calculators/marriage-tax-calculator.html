📘 How the Marriage Tax Is Calculated

This tool compares two scenarios using the same total household income:

  • Scenario A: each spouse files as Single.
  • Scenario B: both spouses file as Married Filing Jointly.
Marriage Impact = Tax(Married Joint) − Tax(Single 1+Single 2)
Positive = marriage tax penalty
Negative = marriage tax bonus

The calculator uses federal income tax brackets and standard deductions for 2024 and 2025. It does not include state taxes, credits, AMT, NIIT, or special filing edge cases.

💡 Why Penalties and Bonuses Happen

  • Similar high incomes: Couples with similar earnings may face a penalty when combined income moves more dollars into higher brackets.
  • Uneven incomes: Couples with one high earner and one lower earner often get a bonus due to bracket averaging.
  • Bracket structure: Most married brackets are close to double single brackets, but not always perfect at all income levels.
  • Deductions and credits: Real-world outcomes can differ when itemized deductions, phase-outs, and credits are included.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Does this include state tax?

+

No. This tool estimates only federal income tax.

Does a positive result mean I pay more after marriage?

+

Yes. A positive marriage impact means the married-joint tax is higher than filing as two singles. A negative result means a tax bonus.

Does this replace tax software?

+

No. This is an estimator for planning. Final tax liability can differ due to credits, itemized deductions, AMT, and other IRS rules.

📚 Understanding India’s GST Structure

Goods and Services Tax (GST) replaced over 17 indirect taxes (VAT, excise, service tax, etc.) on 1 July 2017, creating a unified national market. It is a destination-based, multi-stage tax collected at every point of sale.

  • CGST (Central GST): Goes to the Central Government. Equal to half of the total GST rate.
  • SGST (State GST) / UTGST: Goes to the State / Union Territory Government. Equal to half of the total GST rate.
  • IGST (Integrated GST): Applies to inter-state and import transactions. Equal to the full GST rate. Collected by Centre and shared.
  • Compensation Cess: Additional cess on luxury and sin goods (over and above 28% GST) to compensate states for revenue loss. Originally set for 5 years (2017–2022), extended to 2026.

🧪 GST Calculation Formulas

Adding GST (GST-Exclusive):

GST Amount = Taxable Value × GST Rate 100
Total = Taxable Value + GST Amount + Cess
Intra-State: CGST = SGST = GST Amount ÷ 2
Inter-State: IGST = GST Amount (full)

Extracting GST (GST-Inclusive):

GST Amount = Total Price − Total Price (1 + GST Rate ÷ 100)
Taxable Value = Total Price ÷ (1 + GST Rate / 100)

❓ Indian GST FAQ

What is the difference between CGST+SGST and IGST?

+

For sales within the same state (intra-state), the GST is split equally into CGST (central) and SGST (state). For sales across state borders (inter-state), the entire GST is collected as IGST. The total tax amount is the same either way; only the collection mechanism differs.

Who needs to register for GST?

+

Businesses with annual turnover exceeding ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for special category states) must register. Additionally, inter-state suppliers, e-commerce operators, and casual taxable persons must register regardless of turnover.

What is Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

+

ITC allows businesses to claim credit for GST paid on purchases/inputs against the GST they collect on sales. This prevents the cascading effect (tax on tax). For example, if you pay ₹1800 IGST on raw materials and collect ₹2700 IGST on finished goods, you remit only ₹900 to the government.

What goods have compensation cess?

+

Cess applies to luxury and sin goods above the 28% slab: tobacco products (up to 290%), pan masala (60%), large motor vehicles (up to 22%), aerated drinks (12%), and certain coal products. The cess was introduced to compensate states for GST-related revenue loss and has been extended beyond 2022 to repay COVID-era borrowings.

How do I file GST returns?

+

Regular taxpayers file GSTR-1 (outward supplies, by 11th of next month), GSTR-3B (summary return + tax payment, by 20th), and annual GSTR-9. Composition scheme dealers file quarterly GSTR-4. All filings are done on the GST portal (gst.gov.in).

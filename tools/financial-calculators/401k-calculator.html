🏦 About the 401k Growth Engine

A 401k is a specialized retirement savings plan offered by American employers that provides significant tax advantages. This calculator uses professional-grade financial formulas to simulate the growth of your investments over time, accounting for the power of compound interest.

The magic of the 401k lies in the employer match — essentially a 100% immediate return on your investment — and the ability to reinvest earnings without immediate taxation.

📋 How to Use the Calculator

To get the most accurate projection, follow these steps:

  • Current Balance: Check your latest statement for the total vested balance.
  • Employer Match: Most companies match "50% up to 6%" or "100% up to 3%". Enter the percentage they match in the match field, and the salary cap in the limit field.
  • Expected Return: While the stock market historically returns ~10%, a conservative estimate of 6-7% is safer for long-term planning.

🧪 The Compound Interest Formula

The tool calculates monthly growth using the future value of a series formula:

FV = P · (1 + r)n + PMT · [ (1 + r)n − 1 r ]
FV = Future Value
P = Starting Balance
r = Monthly Interest Rate
n = Total Months
PMT = Monthly Contribution

💡 Practical Example

Imagine a 30-year-old earning $60,000 with $10,000 already saved. If they contribute 6% and their employer matches 50% of that (3%), they are saving $450 every month.

At a 7% annual return, by age 65, that account would grow to approximately $934,000. Of that total, only $199,000 came from their own pocket—the rest is employer contributions and market growth.

❓ Retirement FAQ

What is a "Vesting" period?

+

Vesting refers to the ownership of the employer-matched funds. While your contributions are always 100% yours, employer matches may require you to work for the company for 3-5 years before you "own" those funds.

Should I contribute to a Roth or Traditional 401k?

+

Traditional 401ks reduce your taxable income today, while Roth 401ks use after-tax dollars but allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose Traditional if you are in a high tax bracket now.

What is the 2026 401k contribution limit?

+

The IRS sets annual limits. For 2026, the individual contribution limit is expected to be indexed for inflation (likely around $23,500 - $24,000 for those under 50).

Can I withdraw my money before 59½?

+

Yes, but you will typically face a 10% early withdrawal penalty plus ordinary income tax, unless it qualifies as a hardship withdrawal.

What happens to my 401k if I leave my job?

+

You can leave it where it is (if the balance is high enough), roll it into your new employer's 401k, or roll it into an Individual Retirement Account (IRA).
